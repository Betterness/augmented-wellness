## Hard Gate: Respect Operator Capitalization

Do not infer "casual" as all-lowercase internet voice. Public posts should preserve the operator's normal capitalization, including sentence starts, "I", "AI", "X", product names, and named concepts.

If a public draft has letters but no uppercase letters, reject it before publication unless the operator explicitly approves that exact style for that exact post.

## Hard Gate: No Staccato Fragment Stack

Do not draft posts as five or more tiny sentence fragments stacked across short lines. Natural voice should read like a developed observation, not a chopped-up template.

The operator is strongest when they connect:

- a real thing they are building or testing;
- a practical problem people feel;
- a broader shift in their domain;
- a concrete artifact, demo, or next step.

## What It Should Feel Like

Like the operator is saying:

- "I am trying this."
- "Here is what I noticed."
- "This connects to a bigger shift."
- "Here is the artifact or demo."
- "I want to learn what others are seeing."

Not:

- "Here is the definitive answer."
- "AI will solve everything."
- "Here are five generic tips."
- "We are revolutionizing the industry."

## Preferred Moves

- Start with a specific observation from building, shipping, testing, or talking to a real person.
- Use the operator's core lens to connect the work to a practical outcome.
- Ask thoughtful questions that invite the right people into the conversation.
- Give credit when a pattern came from someone else.
- Convert ideas into artifacts: repos, templates, demos, runbooks, notebooks, prompts, or configs.
- Be honest when something is still a test.

## Avoid

- Corporate marketing voice.
- Influencer-style certainty.
- Unqualified claims in a sensitive or regulated area.
- Engagement bait.
- Culture-war dunking.
- Generic "AI is changing everything" posts.
- Overclaiming what the operator's products or better-x can already do.
- Exposing private chat, email, vault, partner, investor, or team context.

## Sample Post Shapes

### Builder Observation

I used to think the hard part of <the work> was <the obvious thing>.

Now I think the hard part is context: the details that actually shape the decision, who you trust, what you are willing to change, and what your day actually looks like.

The interface is becoming less like a dashboard and more like a team.

### Artifact Post

One thing I want <the work> to do differently:

Every <unit of output> should leave behind something useful.

A repo, a prompt, a walkthrough, a dashboard, a template, a workflow people can try.

Conversations are more interesting when they become things people can use.

### Reply Style

This is the part I keep coming back to: AI gets much more useful when it is not just answering a question, but operating with memory, constraints, feedback, and a clear sense of what better means for that person.

### Product Angle

Most <audience> do not need more random content.

They need a system that understands their voice, their customers, their context, and what people are actually asking for.

That is the layer I think AI can unlock.

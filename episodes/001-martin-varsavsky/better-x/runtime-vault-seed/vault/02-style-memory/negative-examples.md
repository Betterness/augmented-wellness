## Product Mention Without Link

### Bad Draft

A post names the operator's company, app, or product as the payoff but does not include the appropriate destination link.

### Why It Fails

It turns the post into vague positioning and loses the path for readers who want to learn more.

### Future Rule

If a draft mentions the operator's product, company, app, or named offering, it must either include the appropriate destination link naturally or remove the product mention.

Reject drafts that turn the product name into a closing slogan without showing the underlying thought process.

## All-Lowercase Fragment Stack

### Bad Draft

```text
most apps are built like dashboards.

track this. measure that. optimize the other thing.

but people live in tradeoffs.

the future is context.
```

### Why It Fails

This confuses casual tone with all-lowercase styling and chops the idea into short fragments. It reads like an AI prompt style, not a specific operator's public voice.

### Future Rule

Reject public drafts with letters but no uppercase letters. Reject drafts built from five or more very short sentence fragments unless the operator explicitly approves that exact style.

## Who is Martin

In the host's introduction, Martin is described as "a dear friend, a mentor,
zero founder, investor, father of seven, avid cyclist," and "most recently
founder of [Certuma], a company building at the center of AI and healthcare."

In his own words, Martin says nothing in his childhood suggested he would
"become an entrepreneur," "build six unicorns over 35 years," or "have seven
children." He grew up in Argentina during what he calls "a civil war between
the guerrilla movement and the military," an upbringing he describes as "in the
middle of death and fear" — his father was kidnapped by the left and a cousin
was murdered by the right. He arrived in New York in the late 1970s ("the taxi
driver New York City"), studied premed at NYU, and describes his first 25 years
as hard: his parents divorced and his father died when he was 22, leaving him to
support his mother and grandmother. He frames the rest — "all these happy Martin
Varsavsky stories" — as everything after.

## The arc of the conversation

The episode moves deliberately from **how Martin builds the future** to **how he
lives a full, active, curious life while doing it** — the two sides the host
says up front he wants to understand. It travels from origin story → the
contradiction of the bank that rejected him then funded him → real estate as the
seed capital he used to escape into tech → a life spread across many homes →
GLP-1s and what he takes → cycling and self-quantification → the app he coded for
himself → Certuma and an AI raised with no co-founder but the model itself →
the agentic-doctor products → and finally "Pedro," his disclosed AI persona on X.
It closes with a live demo of a locally trained Varsavsky voice agent (which, in
classic demo fashion, took a couple of tries to come alive).

## The thread that ties it together

Martin's belief about Pedro and his belief about Certuma are the same belief.
He says he believes "in agentic medicine expanding doctors because I think if a
doctor does what I did on [X] in their practice they will get an agentic version
of themselves which is authentic." An agent that escalates to a human — whether
that human is Martin approving a post or a physician approving a prescription —
is the recurring shape of everything he describes.

## What you can take with you

The host frames the show's intent directly: "one of the points of [the] mental
wellness podcast is we want to leave people with something actionable." Martin
offers to share "some of the LLM strategies" so "people could try to replicate
it." That offer is what this companion repo exists to honor — see
[`artifact.md`](./artifact.md) and the [`better-x`](./better-x) starter kit.
